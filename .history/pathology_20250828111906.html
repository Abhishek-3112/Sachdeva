A. Hematology

Hematology is the study of blood and its disorders, focusing on diagnosis, treatment, and monitoring.

CBC / Hemogram

A complete blood count (CBC) evaluates red cells, white cells, platelets, and ESR for overall health and disease detection.

  • RBC count, Hemoglobin, Hematocrit, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW
  • WBC count with TLC, DLC, ANC, ALC, AEC, AMC
  • Platelet count & morphology
  • ESR included for inflammation marker

✅ No preparation required

Peripheral Blood Smear

Microscopic evaluation of blood cells for morphology and detection of anemia, malaria, and blood cancers.

Coagulation Profile

  • PT/INR
  • aPTT
  • Bleeding Time & Clotting Time
  • Platelet Count

Used to investigate bleeding disorders, monitor anticoagulant therapy, and assess liver disease.

Hb Electrophoresis / HPLC

Identifies abnormal hemoglobin (Thalassemia, Sickle Cell Disease).

Bone Marrow Aspiration / Biopsy

Cytological/histopathological exam for blood cancers and marrow disorders.

B. Biochemistry

Biochemistry involves chemical analysis for diagnosing and monitoring various systemic diseases.

Liver Function Tests (LFT)

  • ALT, AST, ALP, GGT (enzymes)
  • Albumin, Globulin, Total proteins
  • Total, Direct & Indirect Bilirubin

✅ No preparation required

Renal Function Test (KFT)

Assesses kidney performance using Creatinine, Urea, eGFR, Electrolytes, Calcium/Phosphorus, Uric Acid.

✅ No preparation required

Lipid Profile

Measures total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides, VLDL, and cholesterol ratio to assess heart health.

⚠️ Fasting (8-10 hrs) preferred unless advised otherwise

Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)

TSH, Free T3, Free T4 for hypo/hyperthyroidism diagnosis.

⚠️ Morning fasting preferred; collect before thyroid medication

Diabetes Monitoring

  • Blood Glucose (Fasting, Post-prandial, Random)
  • HbA1c – average blood sugar over 3 months

Cardiac Markers

  • Troponin I/T
  • CK-MB
  • BNP/NT-proBNP

Used in emergency settings for heart attack diagnosis and monitoring.

Tumor Markers

  • PSA – Prostate
  • CA-125 – Ovarian cancer
  • AFP – Liver
  • CEA – Colon

Helpful in diagnosis, prognosis, and monitoring therapy in cancer patients.

C. Serology

  • Diagnosis of infections: HIV, Hepatitis B & C, Typhoid, Malaria, Dengue, COVID-19, Syphilis
  • Blood typing & compatibility testing
  • Autoimmune disease testing (ANA, RA factor, Anti-dsDNA)
  • Vaccine response monitoring (e.g., Hepatitis B surface antibody)

D. Microbiology

Detects pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites) using microscopy, culture, serology, and molecular tests.

  • Urine culture (UTIs)
  • Blood culture (sepsis, bacteremia)
  • Pus, Sputum, Stool cultures
  • Throat & Eye swabs
  • High Vaginal Swab (HVS) for gynecological infections
  • Mycobacterium detection (AFB smear & culture for TB)

E. Cytology & Histopathology

Cytology

Examines individual cells to detect cancer, infections, and abnormalities.

  • Pap Smear – Screening for cervical cancer
  • FNAC (Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology) – Breast, Thyroid, Lymph nodes
  • Fluid cytology – Pleural, Ascitic, CSF

Histopathology

Microscopic study of tissues (biopsies/surgical samples) to diagnose:

  • Cancers (benign vs malignant)
  • Infections (TB, fungal)
  • Inflammatory and autoimmune diseases
