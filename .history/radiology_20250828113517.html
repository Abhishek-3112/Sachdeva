RADIOLOGY & IMAGING SERVICES :

A. Digital X-rays (Radiograph)

Radiography is a type of imaging that uses electromagnetic radiation to view the inside of the body, especially bones and certain tissues. It is a fast, painless, and non-invasive diagnostic tool commonly used in medicine and dentistry. When computers are used to capture and display the images it is called digital radiography.

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we use a High Frequency 600mA Xray Machine from Medtronics and the topend AGFA CR System for the digital X-rays

Safety Note:

X-rays involve ionizing radiation, but the dose is generally very low, especially when high powered x-ray machines are used. The dose further reduces in digital radiography.

Not recommended for pregnant women unless absolutely necessary. It is done after covering the baby with a lead shield.

Common Types of X-rays:

Plain X-rays :
• Chest X-ray : To examine lungs, heart, and chest wall.
• Abdominal X-ray : To check for bowel obstruction, stones or abnormal masses.
• Dental X-rays : To assess teeth, bone loss, cavities, or root problems.
• Bone X-rays : To detect fractures, dislocations, or arthritis.

Preparation: Most plain xrays do not require any preparation and can be done any time. Except plain Xray of the KUB region where you are asked to take a laxative one night prior and come empty stomach the next day after your bowel has nicely cleared

Special X-rays:
Orthopantomogram (OPG) :
Panoramic dental X-ray for full jaw and teeth. Key Features:
• Panoramic View: Captures the entire mouth in a single image.
• Non-invasive: The patient simply stands biting on a mouthpiece; the machine rotates around their head.
• Quick and Painless: Usually takes less than a minute.
• Low Radiation: Relatively low dose compared to other radiographic methods.

Uses of OPG :
• Evaluating tooth development and eruption patterns.
• Detecting impacted teeth (e.g., wisdom teeth).
• Planning for orthodontic treatments.
• Identifying jaw fractures, tumors, cysts, or infections.
• Assessing the bone structure for dental implants.
• Checking for temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders.

Contrast Xray Studies:
• Xray studies after using a contrast medium :
IVP ( intravenous pyelography ), barium studies, HSG (Hysterosalpingography), sonogram / fistulogram

HSG (Hysterosalpingography):

HSG stands for Hysterosalpingography — a special type of X-ray procedure used primarily to evaluate the uterus and fallopian tubes in women, especially in cases of infertility.

When Is It Done?
Typically performed between days 7 and 10 of a woman’s menstrual cycle (after menstruation, before ovulation)

Procedure : A cannula is passed into the cervix and contrast is injected through the cannula and xrays are taken subsequently.

Intravenous Pyelography (IVP):

Intravenous Pyelography (IVP) is a medical imaging technique used to visualize the urinary tract, including the kidneys, ureters, and bladder, by using a contrast dye and X-rays.

Purpose

IVP is typically used to:

• Detect location of Urinary Tract obstruction as in kidney stones or urinary tract tumor
• Investigate frequent urinary tract infections (UTIs)
• Assess structural abnormalities in the urinary tract

Preparation:

• You may be asked to fast for several hours and take a laxative to clear your bowels.
• Injection:
• A contrast dye is injected into a vein, usually in your arm.
• Imaging:
• As the dye travels through your bloodstream to the kidneys and urinary tract, X-rays are taken at timed intervals.
• Observation:
• The dye outlines the urinary structures, allowing doctors to observe their shape, size, and function.

Risks and Considerations

• Allergic reaction to the contrast dye (rare but possible)
• Not ideal for people with poor kidney function or iodine allergy
• Radiation exposure, though generally low

Barium studies

A group of diagnostic imaging tests that use barium sulfate, a contrast material, to help visualize parts of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract.

Types of Barium Studies

Barium Swallow (Esophagogram)
• Examines: Pharynx and esophagus
• Used for: Difficulty swallowing, GERD, strictures, tumors

Barium Meal / Upper GI Series
• Examines: Esophagus, stomach, and duodenum
• Used for: Ulcers, hiatal hernia, gastric tumors

Barium Follow-Through
• Examines: Small intestine
• Used for: Crohn’s disease, malabsorption, small bowel obstruction

Barium Enema (Lower GI Series)
• Examines: Colon and rectum
• Used for: Polyps, diverticulosis, colorectal cancer, IBD

Preparation

• Fasting for several hours (usually overnight)
• For lower GI: Laxatives or enemas to cleanse the colon

B. Ultrasound Scan (also called a Sonogram) :

A non-invasive imaging test that uses high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images of structures inside the body.

How It Works:

A device called a transducer emits sound waves.

These waves bounce off tissues and organs.

The returning echoes are captured and turned into images on a monitor.

Key Features:

No radiation (unlike X-rays or CT scans)

Safe for pregnant women and babies

Provides real-time imaging (great for observing motion, like fetal heartbeat)

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we use the latest technology RS 85 Prestige Whole Body Ultrasound Scan System from SAMSUNG; fully equipped with an array of probes: Convex, High frequency Linear, Intracavitary ( TVS, TRUS) and 3D/4D.

Common Uses of Ultrasound:

Obstetric: Monitor pregnancy, fetal growth, heartbeat, and position.

Pelvic: Evaluate uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, bladder

Abdominal: Examine liver, kidneys, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen

KUB: assess kidneys, urinary bladder, prostate.

Vascular/ Colour Doppler: Check blood flow in arteries and veins

Musculoskeletal: Look at muscles, joints, tendons, or soft tissue masses

Specialized Ultrasounds:

1. 3D/4D Ultrasound: shows organs / fetus in a three dimensional view.

2. Transvaginal/Transrectal Ultrasound: Provides close-up internal views using special probes.

3. Color Doppler: A specialized type of ultrasound imaging that shows the flow of blood through blood vessels — including arteries and veins — using color-coded visuals.

Purpose of a Color Doppler:
• Assess blood flow direction, speed, and volume.
• Detect blockages, narrowing or clots.
• Check fetal blood circulation in pregnancy
• Monitor organ perfusion (e.g., kidneys, liver, testicles)

Common Uses:
• Deep vein thrombosis (DVT)
• Varicose Veins
• Carotid artery evaluation (stroke risk)
• Pregnancy: check placental and fetal blood flow
• Liver or kidney transplant assessment
• Testicular torsion evaluation

4. Shearwave Ultrasound (Shearwave Elastography)

Shear Wave Elastography (SWE) is an advanced, non-invasive imaging technique that measures tissue stiffness using ultrasound-generated shear waves. It’s especially useful for assessing liver fibrosis, tumors, and muscle conditions.

How It Works

• The ultrasound probe sends acoustic pulses into the tissue.
• These pulses generate shear waves
• The machine tracks how fast these waves move through the tissue:
• Faster waves = stiffer tissue
• Slower waves = softer tissue
• The result is a color-coded map of stiffness (an elastogram) over a conventional ultrasound image.

Common Uses

• Liver: ( Fibroscan) Assess fibrosis and cirrhosis (e.g., hepatitis B/C, NAFLD)
• Breast: Alternative to liver biopsy in many cases. Distinguish benign vs. malignant lesions
• Thyroid: Evaluate suspicious nodules
• Prostate: Guide biopsies, assess stiffness in cancer-suspicious regions
• Muscles & Tendons: Detect strain, tear, or scar tissue
• Lymph nodes & tumors: benign vs malignant

5. Fat Quantification (QUS) :

It assesses the amount of fat in the liver by two techniques: TAI (tissue attenuation index) called by various names like CAP or UGAP value and TSI ( tissue scattering index ) and then combines the two to give a Fat Fraction % (UDFF).

Shearwave Ultrasound and Fat Quantification are used together to study the liver. While most cetres just give the tissue attenuation value (CAP / UGAP), at Sachdeva Diagnostics we use the top end machines which gives the Fat Fraction ( UDFF% ) also along with the tissue attenuation value.
