Hematology

Hematology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the study of blood, blood-forming organs, and blood diseases. It involves diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of a wide range of conditions related to blood and its components:

  • Red Blood Cells (RBCs): Carry oxygen to the body's tissues.
  • White Blood Cells (WBCs): Fight infections.
  • Platelets: Help with blood clotting.
  • Plasma: Liquid portion of blood that carries cells & proteins.

Common Tests and Procedures in Hematology

a) CBC / Hemogram

A Hemogram test, also known as a Complete Blood Count (CBC), is a comprehensive blood analysis that evaluates various blood components. It helps in assessing overall health, diagnosing anemia, infections, blood disorders, and monitoring treatment effectiveness.

What it checks:

  • Red Blood Cells (RBCs): Hemoglobin, hematocrit, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW.
  • White Blood Cells (WBCs): TLC, DLC, Absolute counts (ANC, ALC, AEC, AMC).
  • Platelets: Number & morphology, essential for clotting.
  • ESR: Marker of inflammation.

No preparation is needed for CBC.

At Sachdeva Diagnostics: All CBC tests are done using a fully automated hematology analyzer, with manual smear examination by a Pathologist for accuracy. ESR is included in CBC for every patient.

b) Peripheral Blood Smear

A microscopic analysis of blood cells by a Pathologist. Provides complete morphological details of cells, helping in diagnosing anemia, infections (e.g., malaria), and blood cancers.

c) Coagulation Tests

A Coagulation Profile checks the blood‚Äôs ability to clot. It helps in diagnosing bleeding or clotting disorders.

Includes:

  • Prothrombin Time (PT) & INR
  • Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT)
  • Platelet Count
  • Bleeding Time (BT)
  • Clotting Time (CT)

Why is it done?

  • Investigate unexplained bleeding or bruising
  • Assess bleeding risk before surgery
  • Monitor patients on anticoagulants (e.g., Warfarin)
  • Diagnose clotting disorders (Hemophilia, von Willebrand disease, DIC)
  • Evaluate liver disease (liver produces clotting proteins)

d) Hb Electrophoresis / HPLC

Measures the percentage of various types of Hemoglobin. Helps in diagnosing Thalassemia, Sickle Cell Disease.

e) Bone Marrow Aspiration / Biopsy

Cytological / histopathological examination of the bone marrow. Helps diagnose blood & bone marrow disorders, including cancers.

Liver Function Tests (LFT)

Assess liver health and functioning by measuring specific substances it produces or processes.

  • Enzymes: ALT, AST, ALP, GGT (earliest marker of liver disease)
  • Proteins: Albumin, Globulin, Total protein
  • Waste Products: Total, direct & indirect bilirubin

Purpose: Monitoring liver disease, screening for hepatitis, evaluating medication side-effects.

Preparation: No preparation required.

Renal Function Test (KFT)

Evaluates kidney health and efficiency.

  • Creatinine, Urea, BUN
  • BUN/Creatinine ratio ‚Üí Identifies cause of kidney malfunction
  • eGFR ‚Üí Estimates blood filtration efficiency
  • Electrolytes: Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, Ionic Calcium
  • Total Proteins, Albumin, Globulin, A/G ratio
  • Calcium & Phosphorus (detects bone & cardiovascular risks in CKD)
  • Uric Acid

Purpose: Ordered in cases of swelling, urine changes, cramps, weight fluctuations, or in patients with diabetes, hypertension, or heart disease.

Preparation: No preparation required.

Lipid Profile

Measures types of fats in blood to assess cardiovascular risks.

  • Total cholesterol, LDL ("bad cholesterol"), HDL ("good cholesterol")
  • Triglycerides, VLDL
  • Cholesterol/HDL ratio ‚Üí Higher ratio = higher risk
  • Non-HDL cholesterol ‚Üí all harmful cholesterol

Purpose: Screens for risk of heart attack & stroke (esp. in obese, sedentary, smokers, or those with family history).

Preparation: Overnight fasting (8‚Äì10 hrs) is preferred.

Thyroid Function Tests (TFT)

Assess thyroid gland functioning to diagnose hypo/hyperthyroidism.

  • TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
  • Free T3
  • Free T4

Preparation: Morning fasting sample preferred. If on thyroid meds, sample before taking medicine.

Serology

Serology is the scientific study of blood serum with regard to the immune system's response to pathogens. It plays a crucial role in medical diagnostics by detecting antibodies or antigens in a patient‚Äôs blood.

Types of Antibodies Detected

  • IgM ‚Äì Indicates recent or acute infection.
  • IgG ‚Äì Indicates past exposure or long-term immunity.
  • IgA ‚Äì Crucial for mucosal immunity (respiratory & digestive system).
  • IgE ‚Äì Crucial for allergic diseases.

Main Applications

  1. Diagnosis of infections

    Detects antibodies (IgM, IgG) against viruses, bacteria, fungi, or parasites. Commonly used in HIV, Hepatitis, Typhoid, Malaria, COVID-19, Syphilis, and more.

  2. Blood typing and compatibility testing

    Identifies blood groups (A, B, AB, O) and Rh factor (positive/negative). Essential for blood transfusion and organ transplantation.

  3. Autoimmune disease testing

    Detects autoantibodies such as ANA (antinuclear antibodies) for SLE and Rheumatoid Factor for Rheumatoid Arthritis.

  4. Vaccine response monitoring

    Measures antibody levels post-vaccination (e.g., COVID-19, Hepatitis B) to confirm immunity.

Microbiology

Microbiology in medical diagnostics is a vital branch of laboratory medicine focused on detecting and identifying microorganisms (bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites) that cause infectious diseases. It supports diagnosis, treatment, infection control, and prevention.

Key Roles in Medical Diagnostics

  1. Detection of Pathogens

    Identifies bacterial, viral, fungal, and parasitic infections such as tuberculosis, COVID-19, UTIs, malaria, sepsis, etc.

    Methods of detection:

    • Microscopy: Direct visualization using stains (e.g., Gram stain, Ziehl-Neelsen, KOH prep).
    • Serology: Detects antibodies/antigens (e.g., HIV, Hepatitis B).
    • Molecular Techniques: PCR, RT-PCR for DNA/RNA detection (COVID-19, TB, HPV).
    • Rapid Antigen Tests: Quick detection (influenza, malaria).
    • Culture: Growing samples in special media to identify pathogens & test drug susceptibility.
  2. Antimicrobial Susceptibility Testing (AST)

    Determines which antibiotics or drugs are effective against a specific pathogen. Supports appropriate therapy and helps prevent antibiotic resistance.

    Common Culture & Sensitivity Tests:

    • Urine culture: Detects urinary tract infections (UTIs).
    • Blood culture: Identifies bloodstream infections (bacteremia, fungemia).
    • Pus culture: Used for wound/skin and soft tissue infections.
    • Sputum culture: Detects respiratory infections.
    • Stool culture: Identifies gastrointestinal infections.
    • Throat swab culture: Detects throat infections.
    • Conjunctival swab culture: Used for eye infections.
    • HVS (High Vaginal Swab) test & culture: Used in gynecology to detect infections like bacterial vaginosis and vaginal thrush.

Cytology & Histopathology

Cytology is the study of individual cells to detect cancer, precancerous lesions, and infections by examining cell size, shape, and organization.

Main Purposes

  • Early detection of cancers (cervical, lung, breast).
  • Screening for precancerous lesions.
  • Diagnosis of infections (viral, fungal, bacterial).

Advantages

  • Minimally or non-invasive.
  • Quick results.
  • Cost-effective and widely accessible.
  • Helps in early detection of malignancy & infections.

a) Exfoliative Cytology

Cells naturally shed or collected from epithelial surfaces.

Example: Cervical Pap Smear

  • Detects abnormal/pre-cancerous cells.
  • Helps diagnose infections (HPV, viral, fungal).

Procedure: Cells collected from cervix using sterile brush/spatula, examined microscopically. Avoid intercourse, tampons, or creams 1‚Äì2 days before test.

ICMR Guideline: Women ‚â•21 yrs ‚Üí every 3 yrs. With HPV test ‚Üí every 5 yrs.

b) Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC)

Cells aspirated with a fine needle from lumps or masses.

Purpose: To determine benign vs malignant lumps.

  • Breast lumps, thyroid nodules.
  • Enlarged lymph nodes, salivary gland swellings.
  • Superficial & deeper lumps (USG-guided for non-palpable).

Benefits: Minimally invasive, quick, inexpensive, outpatient-based. No preparation required.

c) Fluid Cytology

Examines cells in body fluids:

  • Pleural fluid (chest), Peritoneal fluid (abdomen).
  • CSF (brain/spinal cord).
  • Urine cytology for bladder cancer.
  • Semen analysis.

Histopathology is the microscopic study of tissues to detect cancers, infections, inflammatory, and systemic diseases.

Process

  • Tissue obtained via biopsy/surgery.
  • Preserved in formalin, embedded in paraffin.
  • Thin slicing (4‚Äì5¬µm) with microtome.
  • Staining with H&E for visualization.
  • Microscopic examination by Pathologist.
  • Immunohistochemistry (IHC) for antigen detection.

Applications

  • Diagnosis of cancers.
  • Detection of infections & inflammatory diseases (e.g., nephritis, hepatitis).
  • Evaluation of transplanted organs.
  • Forensic pathology.
