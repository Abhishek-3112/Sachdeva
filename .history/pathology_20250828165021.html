A. Hematology

Hematology is the study of blood and its disorders, focusing on diagnosis, treatment, and monitoring.

CBC / Hemogram

A complete blood count (CBC) evaluates red cells, white cells, platelets, and ESR for overall health and disease detection.

  • RBC count, Hemoglobin, Hematocrit, MCV, MCH, MCHC, RDW
  • WBC count with TLC, DLC, ANC, ALC, AEC, AMC
  • Platelet count & morphology
  • ESR included for inflammation marker

✅ No preparation required

B. Biochemistry

Biochemistry involves chemical analysis for diagnosing and monitoring various systemic diseases.

Liver Function Tests (LFT)

  • ALT, AST, ALP, GGT (enzymes)
  • Albumin, Globulin, Total proteins
  • Total, Direct & Indirect Bilirubin

✅ No preparation required

C. Serology

  • Diagnosis of infections: HIV, Hepatitis B & C, Typhoid, Malaria, Dengue, COVID-19, Syphilis
  • Blood typing & compatibility testing
  • Autoimmune disease testing (ANA, RA factor, Anti-dsDNA)
  • Vaccine response monitoring (e.g., Hepatitis B surface antibody)

D. Microbiology

Detects pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites) using microscopy, culture, serology, and molecular tests.

  • Urine culture (UTIs)
  • Blood culture (sepsis, bacteremia)
  • Pus, Sputum, Stool cultures
  • Throat & Eye swabs
  • High Vaginal Swab (HVS) for gynecological infections
  • Mycobacterium detection (AFB smear & culture for TB)

E. Cytology & Histopathology

Cytology

Examines individual cells to detect cancer, infections, and abnormalities.

  • Pap Smear – Screening for cervical cancer
  • FNAC – Breast, Thyroid, Lymph nodes
  • Fluid cytology – Pleural, Ascitic, CSF
