2. RADIOLOGY & IMAGING SERVICES :

A. Digital X-rays (Radiograph)
Radiography is a type of imaging that uses electromagnetic radiation to view the inside of the body, especially bones and certain tissues. It is a fast, painless, and non-invasive diagnostic tool commonly used in medicine and dentistry. When computers are used to capture and display the images it is called digital radiography.

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we use a High Frequency 600mA Xray Machine from Medtronics and the topend AGFA CR System for the digital X-rays

Safety Note:
- X-rays involve ionizing radiation, but the dose is generally very low, especially when high powered x-ray machines are used. The dose further reduces in digital radiography.
- Not recommended for pregnant women unless absolutely necessary. It is done after covering the baby with a lead shield.

Common Types of X-rays:

1. Plain X-rays :
• Chest X-ray : To examine lungs, heart, and chest wall.
• Abdominal X-ray : To check for bowel obstruction, stones or abnormal masses.
• Dental X-rays : To assess teeth, bone loss, cavities, or root problems.
• Bone X-rays : To detect fractures, dislocations, or arthritis.
Preparation: Most plain xrays do not require any preparation and can be done any time. Except plain Xray of the KUB region where you are asked to take a laxative one night prior and come empty stomach the next day after your bowel has nicely cleared

2. Special X-rays:
Orthopantomogram (OPG):
Panoramic dental X-ray for full jaw and teeth. Key Features:
• Panoramic View: Captures the entire mouth in a single image.
• Non-invasive: The patient simply stands biting on a mouthpiece; the machine rotates around their head.
• Quick and Painless: Usually takes less than a minute.
• Low Radiation: Relatively low dose compared to other radiographic methods.

Uses of OPG :
• Evaluating tooth development and eruption patterns.
• Detecting impacted teeth (e.g., wisdom teeth).
• Planning for orthodontic treatments.
• Identifying jaw fractures, tumors, cysts, or infections.
• Assessing the bone structure for dental implants.
• Checking for temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders.

3. Contrast Xray Studies:
• Xray studies after using a contrast medium : IVP (intravenous pyelography), barium studies, HSG (Hysterosalpingography), sonogram / fistulogram

HSG (Hysterosalpingography): a special type of X-ray procedure used primarily to evaluate the uterus and fallopian tubes in women, especially in cases of infertility.
When Is It Done? Typically performed between days 7 and 10 of a woman’s menstrual cycle (after menstruation, before ovulation)
Procedure: A cannula is passed into the cervix and contrast is injected through the cannula and xrays are taken subsequently.
At Sachdeva Diagnostics the procedure is done by Dr Savita Sachdeva who has a vast experience of doing nearly five thousand HSG procedures in the last 20 years.

Intravenous Pyelography (IVP): a medical imaging technique used to visualize the urinary tract, including the kidneys, ureters, and bladder, by using a contrast dye and X-rays.
Purpose:
• Detect location of Urinary Tract obstruction as in kidney stones or urinary tract tumor
• Investigate frequent urinary tract infections (UTIs)
• Assess structural abnormalities in the urinary tract

Preparation:
• You may be asked to fast for several hours and take a laxative to clear your bowels.
• A contrast dye is injected into a vein, usually in your arm.
• As the dye travels through your bloodstream to the kidneys and urinary tract, X-rays are taken at timed intervals.
• The dye outlines the urinary structures, allowing doctors to observe their shape, size, and function.
Risks: Allergic reaction to the dye, not for poor kidney function or iodine allergy, radiation exposure though generally low.

Barium Studies: imaging tests using barium sulfate for the GI tract.
Types:
1. Barium Swallow – Pharynx & esophagus
2. Barium Meal / Upper GI Series – Esophagus, stomach, duodenum
3. Barium Follow-Through – Small intestine
4. Barium Enema – Colon & rectum
Preparation: Fasting for several hours, laxatives/enemas for lower GI.

B. Ultrasound Scan (Sonogram)

A non-invasive imaging test using high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images.

How It Works: Transducer emits sound waves → echoes return → machine creates image.

Key Features:
• No radiation
• Safe for pregnant women and babies
• Provides real-time imaging

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we use the latest RS 85 Prestige Whole Body Ultrasound Scan System from SAMSUNG, with Convex, Linear, Intracavitary (TVS, TRUS), and 3D/4D probes.

Common Uses:
Obstetric, Pelvic, Abdominal, KUB, Vascular/Colour Doppler, Musculoskeletal.

Specialized Ultrasounds:
1. 3D/4D Ultrasound
2. Transvaginal/Transrectal
3. Color Doppler – blood flow visualization
4. Shearwave Ultrasound (Elastography) – assess liver, breast, thyroid, prostate, muscles, tumors
5. Fat Quantification (QUS) – measures liver fat (TAI + TSI, gives Fat Fraction %)

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we provide both Fat Fraction (UDFF%) and tissue attenuation (CAP/UGAP), unlike most centres.
