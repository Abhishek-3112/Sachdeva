A. Digital X-rays (Radiograph)

Radiography is a type of imaging that uses electromagnetic radiation to view the inside of the body, especially bones and certain tissues. It is a fast, painless, and non-invasive diagnostic tool commonly used in medicine and dentistry. When computers are used to capture and display the images it is called digital radiography.

At Sachdeva Diagnostics we use a High Frequency 600mA Xray Machine from Medtronics and the topend AGFA CR System for the digital X-rays.

Safety Note:

  • X-rays involve ionizing radiation, but the dose is very low, especially with high powered machines.
  • Digital radiography further reduces exposure.
  • Not recommended for pregnant women unless necessary (with lead shield).

Common Types of X-rays:

  • Chest X-ray: Examine lungs, heart, chest wall
  • Abdominal X-ray: Check bowel obstruction, stones, masses
  • Dental X-rays: Teeth, bone loss, cavities
  • Bone X-rays: Fractures, dislocations, arthritis

Preparation: Most plain X-rays don’t require preparation. Except X-ray of the KUB region where a laxative is taken one night prior and patient comes empty stomach the next morning.

Special X-rays: Orthopantomogram (OPG)

Panoramic dental X-ray for full jaw and teeth.

  • Panoramic View: Entire mouth in single image
  • Non-invasive: Patient stands while machine rotates
  • Quick & Painless: Takes less than a minute
  • Low Radiation: Safer than many other methods

Uses: Tooth development, impacted teeth, orthodontics, fractures, TMJ disorders, implants.

Contrast X-ray Studies:

X-ray studies after contrast medium: IVP, barium studies, HSG, sonogram / fistulogram

HSG (Hysterosalpingography)

Special X-ray for uterus & fallopian tubes in infertility cases. Done between day 7-10 of cycle.

Procedure: Cannula inserted in cervix → contrast injected → X-rays taken. At Sachdeva Diagnostics, performed by Dr Savita Sachdeva with 20+ years experience (5000+ cases).

Intravenous Pyelography (IVP)

Visualizes urinary tract (kidneys, ureters, bladder).

  • Detect obstruction (stones, tumor)
  • Investigate frequent UTIs
  • Assess abnormalities

Preparation: Fasting, laxative, contrast injection, X-rays at intervals.

Risks: Rare allergy to dye, avoid in poor kidney function, low radiation dose.

Barium Studies

  • Barium Swallow: Pharynx & esophagus
  • Barium Meal / Upper GI: Esophagus, stomach, duodenum
  • Barium Follow-through: Small intestine
  • Barium Enema: Colon & rectum

Preparation: Overnight fasting, laxatives/enemas for lower GI.

B. Ultrasound Scan (Sonogram)

A non-invasive imaging test using high-frequency sound waves to produce real-time images.

  • No radiation, safe in pregnancy
  • Provides real-time imaging

At Sachdeva Diagnostics, we use Samsung RS 85 Prestige with Convex, Linear, Intracavitary, and 3D/4D probes.

Common Uses of Ultrasound:

  • Obstetric: Pregnancy, fetal growth
  • Pelvic: Uterus, ovaries, bladder
  • Abdominal: Liver, kidneys, pancreas
  • KUB: Kidneys, bladder, prostate
  • Vascular/Colour Doppler: Blood flow in veins/arteries
  • MSK: Muscles, joints, tendons

Specialized Ultrasounds:

  • 3D/4D Ultrasound: 3D view of fetus/organs
  • Transvaginal/Transrectal: Internal close-ups
  • Color Doppler: Blood flow assessment

Shearwave Ultrasound (Elastography)

Measures tissue stiffness via shear waves. Useful in liver, breast, thyroid, prostate, muscles, tumors.

Fat Quantification (QUS)

Assesses liver fat by combining TAI (CAP/UGAP) + TSI to give Fat Fraction % (UDFF). At Sachdeva Diagnostics, we provide UDFF% unlike most centres.
