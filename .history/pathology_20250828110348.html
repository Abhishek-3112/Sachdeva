Pathology & Laboratory Services

A. Hematology

Hematology is the branch of medicine that focuses on the study of blood, the blood-forming organs, and blood diseases...

  • Red Blood Cells (RBCs) – Carry oxygen to the body's tissues.
  • White Blood Cells (WBCs) – Fight infection.
  • Platelets – Help with blood clotting.
  • Plasma – The liquid portion of blood that carries cells and proteins.

Common Tests and Procedures in Hematology

a) CBC / Hemogram :

Hemogram test also known as a complete blood count (CBC)...

  • Red blood cells (RBCs): Measures the number of RBCs and their characteristics.
  • White blood cells (WBCs): Counts total and types of WBCs (TLC, DLC, ANC, etc).
  • Platelets: Checks the number and morphology of platelets.
  • ESR: An important marker of inflammation.

At Sachdeva Diagnostics every CBC is cross-checked with manual slide review for accuracy.

b) Peripheral Blood Smear :

Microscopic analysis of blood cells, useful for anaemia, malaria, cancers.

c) Coagulation Tests :

Includes PT/INR, aPTT, BT, CT, Platelet count for clotting assessment.

d) Hb Electrophoresis / HPLC :

Used for thalassemia, sickle cell disease.

e) Bone Marrow Aspiration / Biopsy :

Cytological/histopathological study for blood and marrow disorders.

B. Biochemistry

Biochemistry involves chemical analysis of blood, serum, plasma, urine and body fluids for diagnosis and monitoring.

Common Biochemistry Tests

  • Blood glucose – Fasting, Postprandial, Random, HbA1c.
  • Liver Function Tests – Bilirubin, SGOT, SGPT, ALP, GGT, Proteins, Albumin, Globulin.
  • Kidney Function Tests – Urea, Creatinine, Uric Acid, Electrolytes.
  • Lipid Profile – Cholesterol, Triglycerides, HDL, LDL, VLDL, Ratios.
  • Thyroid Function Tests – T3, T4, TSH, Free T3/T4, Anti-TPO antibodies.
  • Hormone Assays – Cortisol, Insulin, Prolactin, LH, FSH, Testosterone, Estrogen.
  • Tumor Markers – PSA, CEA, CA-125, CA-19.9, AFP, β-hCG.
  • Cardiac Markers – Troponin I/T, CK-MB, BNP, Pro-BNP.
  • Vitamins & Minerals – Vitamin D, Vitamin B12, Calcium, Phosphorus, Iron Studies.
  • Toxicology – Alcohol, Drugs, Heavy Metals.

C. Serology

Serology deals with the study of serum and immune responses in body fluids. It is used for diagnosing infections and immune disorders.

Common Serology Tests

  • HIV 1 & 2 Testing (ELISA, Rapid)
  • Hepatitis B (HBsAg), Hepatitis C (Anti-HCV)
  • Widal Test for Typhoid
  • Dengue NS1 Antigen, Dengue IgM/IgG
  • Malaria Antigen Test
  • VDRL / RPR for Syphilis
  • ASO Titre, CRP, Rheumatoid Factor
  • ANA, dsDNA (Autoimmune Disorders)

D. Microbiology

Microbiology involves studying infectious agents like bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.

Common Microbiology Tests

  • Culture and Sensitivity (Blood, Urine, Sputum, Pus, Throat Swab, Stool, etc.)
  • AFB Staining & Culture for Tuberculosis
  • KOH Mount for Fungal Infections
  • Gram Stain for Bacterial Identification
  • Stool Examination for Parasites

E. Cytology

Cytology is the microscopic examination of cells to diagnose diseases.

Common Cytology Procedures

  • Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology (FNAC) – Thyroid, Breast, Lymph Node, etc.
  • Pap Smear – For detection of cervical cancer and precancerous conditions.
  • Fluid Cytology – Pleural, Ascitic, Peritoneal, Pericardial, CSF.
  • Urine Cytology – For detection of urinary tract cancers.

F. Histopathology

Histopathology is the microscopic examination of tissue samples to study disease at the cellular level.

Common Histopathology Services

  • Biopsy Examination – Skin, Breast, GI Tract, Lung, Kidney, Liver, etc.
  • Surgical Pathology – Study of surgically removed tissue samples.
  • Special Stains – For TB, Fungal Infections, and Other Specific Pathologies.
  • Immunohistochemistry (IHC) – For cancer diagnosis and classification.
